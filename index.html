Alice

Within NeoWare, ALICE operates in the realm of Spatial Computing, focusing on Augmented Reality (AR). Broadening the scope in which we view education, ALICE creates a variety of resources that allow users to create their own AR-experiences.

The underlying messages behind on-the-job training, marketing, sales, or even basic communication is built around one common goal: educating. Augmented Reality adds a deep layer of engagement that solidifies knowledge retention, educating users through a new experiential dimension.

Neosight

Combining the two pillars of NeoWare, NeoSight focuses on obtaining government contracts in the arena of simulation and data security. Augmented reality permits the transfer of data, creating a link between field agents utilizing AR-enabled devices and the databases back in the home office, facilitating highly impactful interactions. Our technological advances utilizing blockchain technology has raised the bar for secure data transfer to new heights.

A strong strategic partnership with the University of Central Florida and neighboring companies (such as Raytheon, Lockheed Martin, and Riptide Software) positions NeoSight to execute on high-level government contracts.

NeoPak

Components of the NeoWare DCM System include major advances and improvements on present-day technology. The ZIP file standard was conceived in 1991 to provide a standard method of packaging files and directories into a single file primarily for distribution over the internet. NeoPak will build upon this functionality by introducing blockchain supported secure Public Key Encryption, Identity Management, Digital Rights Management, and Zero Knowledge Proof support. These capabilities are important for support of a decentralized web (Web 3.0) where user information and proprietary data is directly controlled by the user.
